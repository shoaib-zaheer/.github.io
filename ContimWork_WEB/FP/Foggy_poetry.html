Foggy Poetry

A collection of feelings, emotions, or philosophical writings by different people—individuals with varying mentalities and beliefs, spanning an undefined age gap, irrespective of gender, and hailing from regions that encompass more than just the western or eastern hemisphere, but from all around the globe. The shared element among all these writers is their focus on the same topic.

Now the question arises: can we categorize these diverse individual writings as a poetic approach or poem? Does this style of writing encapsulate the entire intention of poetry? In this case, (Foggy poetry).

Foggy poetry is an idea of writing poems, but not precisely composing traditional poems. Instead, it involves the composition of texts or separate lines of writing coming together in a poetic rhythm under one overarching topic or heading—a bouquet decorated with different flowers, each with unique colors and smells. The idea emerged while I was surfing the internet on various platforms and reading what people are sharing. I considered the viewers' responses as comments to those shared contents. The primary attraction in internet circuses lies in reading people's comments—how and what these lines of text murmur about posts, profiles, or videos, presented in different writing styles or from diverse angles of thinking.
